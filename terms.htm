Confused with college terms?

We can help you!

Applying

  • Early Decision : Early Decision is binding which means if any student applies in Early Decision and gets accepted, he/she is obliged (compulsory) to attend that college. A student cannot apply to more than 1 college under an early decision plan. The deadline for Early Decision for most colleges is during November and you get the decision by December. If you plan to apply for Early Decision, College should be your first-choice college. Every college does not provide Early Decision plans, so you should check your college website to find it out.
  • Early Action: Early Action is Non-binding which means students apply to the colleges Early(by the end of November ) and also get the decision Early(by December). It is similar to Early Decision; however, you can apply to as many colleges as you want in early action, unlike early decision which only allows applying to one college. . Every college does not provide Early Action plans, so you should check your college website to find it out. We knew that Early Decision and Early Action have Early Deadlines. But what if you cannot apply Early? No worries, you have options for other admission plans!
  • Regular Decision : This is the most common type of decision students apply for. For this, you have to apply to colleges by deadlines and colleges usually have Regular Decision Deadlines in April. In Regular Decision, often termed as “RD”, you can apply to as many colleges as you want. The colleges wait for all students to apply by deadlines, evaluate the applications, and give you the decision. So, the decision for early decision requires a little extra time. If you cannot apply for Early Decision or Early Action, This plan can be the best for you. Almost Every college has Regular Decision plans.
  • Rolling Admission : With Rolling Admission, the deadline is the same as the regular decision deadline. The only difference is that colleges that offer rolling admissions do not wait till after the deadline to send out acceptance letters. They start evaluating the applications as they come in and send out letters as soon as they’ve made a decision. So, if you apply for Rolling Admission, you will get the decision in weeks.

Scholarships

  • Need-Based Scholarship : Need-based scholarships are offered based on your financial need. Unlike Merit Scholarship, Need-Based scholarship does not depend upon the SAT score or GPA. The college asks you the amount of scholarship you need, and if you get accepted, they provided you this scholarship. Every college does not provide Need-Based scholarships to International students and mostly, this scholarship is provided by private universities(Want to know about Public and private universities, look below) in the USA. As an international student, it is difficult to receive a Need-based scholarship as compared to Merit scholarships.
  • Merit Scholarhip : Merit Scholarships are offered based on your SAT scores, GPA, Leadership roles, Involvement in Community scholarships. The greater your SAT score and GPA, the higher the merit scholarships. Almost every college provides Merit scholarships, so, you should visit the college website to find out about it or visit our scholarship section on our website.

General Terms

  • WES(World Education Service) : You might have heard that some colleges require WES from International students to apply.WES provides Credentials evaluations for International students planning to study in the USA. You will have to courier the stamped photocopy of your transcripts stamped with the official source(can verify in USEF or Sanothimi) to the WES, and WES will evaluate your transcripts and will directly send your transcripts to the college you want. Most colleges do not require WES, so go to your University website and check whether they require WES. The process for WES can be complex, so if you want guidance in this process, you can contact us!
  • CSS Profile : In case if you are applying for Need-based Aid, you should provide a CSS profile to College.CSS profile is an electronic form provided by the college board where you mention your family income, the amount of Need based scholarship you want, and other personal information. It takes 25$ to send a CSS profile to college via Collegeboard and 16$ for each additional college.
  • ISFAA : What if you are applying for a Need-Based scholarship and you are not able to pay 16$ each time you fill the CSS profile. For that, there is an alternative form for international students called ISFAA-International Student Financial Aid Application. ISFAA is similar to CSS profile, but it is in paper form and you should not pay any amount to send it. You can print the form, fill it, and email it to your university. Every University does not accept ISFAA, so you have to ask your admission officer if you can send ISFFA in place of CSS profile.
  • Common Application : Common Application is a Platform Which you can use to apply to different colleges in the USA with the same application. There are more than 900 colleges in Common Application and the main benefit is if you apply via Common Application, your application fee can be waived. So, we highly recommend you to apply to your college via a common application if your college is in Common Application. If your college is not in Common Application, you have to apply them from their respective website.
  • COA(Cost of Attendance) : Cost of attendance is the estimated annual cost of attending a particular college or university. It includes tuition, room and board, books, supplies, transportation, loan fees, and miscellaneous expenses.
  • EFC(Expected Family contribution) : EFC is a term that is used to analyze the amount of money you or your family can pay to the college. If you mention that your EFC is 12000$, College understands that you have the ability to pay 12000$ per year to college.
  • In-state tuition refers to the rate paid by students with a permanent residence in the state in which their university is located. Out-of-state tuition refers to the rate that students coming from outside the state, including international students, pay to attend a public state school. Since we are International students, we are considered as an Out of state students and out-of-state-tuition applies to us.
  • COF(Certificate of Finance) : It is the form that you have to submit to your College which helps colleges and universities obtain complete and accurate information about the funds available to international applicants who want to study in the United States. It is free of cost and if your University asks for it, just fill it and email it to the concerned person.
